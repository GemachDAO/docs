# 🏦 What is DeFi?

**DeFi** stands for **Decentralized Finance** — a broad term for financial services that run on the blockchain, without any bank, broker, or institution in the middle.

Think about everything you can do with a bank: save money, borrow money, trade currencies, earn interest. DeFi does all of that — but the rules are written in code, not set by humans, and anyone in the world can use them without needing approval.

## How does DeFi work? Smart contracts.

The engine behind DeFi is something called a **smart contract** — a program that lives on the blockchain and runs automatically when certain conditions are met.

Here's a simple analogy: imagine a vending machine. You put in money, select your item, and the machine dispenses it — no cashier needed, no discretion involved. Smart contracts work the same way: once the code is deployed, it executes exactly as written, every time, for anyone who interacts with it.

This means:
- **No middlemen** — you interact with code directly
- **No downtime** — smart contracts run 24/7
- **No discrimination** — anyone with a wallet can use them
- **Transparent** — the rules are public and auditable

## Key DeFi activities explained

### 🔄 Trading / Swapping

The most common DeFi activity. **Swapping** means exchanging one token for another — like a currency exchange bureau, but instant, 24/7, and without any broker taking a large cut.

On GDex Pro, you can swap hundreds of token pairs across multiple blockchains with a few clicks.

### 💰 Lending and Borrowing

In DeFi, you can **lend** your crypto to earn interest, or **borrow** crypto by putting up other crypto as collateral — all without a credit check or bank approval.

**Gemach's lending platform is [GLend](../products/glend/README.md)** — it lets you supply assets to earn yield or borrow against your holdings algorithmically.

### 🌉 Bridging

Different blockchains (Ethereum, Solana, BNB Chain, etc.) are separate networks that don't natively talk to each other. **Bridging** is the process of moving tokens from one blockchain to another.

GDex Pro has a built-in [Bridge feature](../products/gdex-pro/features/bridge-functionality.md) that handles this for you, supporting transfers across Ethereum, Base, BNB Chain, Solana, Arbitrum, Optimism, Sui, and more.

### 🌾 Yield Farming

When you provide liquidity to a DeFi protocol (essentially lending your tokens to a pool so others can trade against them), you earn **yield** — rewards paid in tokens for your contribution.

**[GVault](../products/gvault.md)** is Gemach's yield product, designed to help you earn returns on your holdings.

### 📈 Perpetual Trading

DeFi also enables **perpetual futures trading** — leveraged trading on token prices without an expiry date. This is more advanced and carries higher risk, but GDex Pro supports it for users who want it. See [Perpetual Trading](../products/gdex-pro/perpetual-trading-perp.md) for more.

## DeFi vs traditional finance (TradFi)

| | Traditional Finance | DeFi |
|---|---|---|
| **Hours** | Business hours | 24/7/365 |
| **Access** | Requires bank account, ID | Anyone with a wallet |
| **Control** | Bank holds your funds | You hold your funds |
| **Fees** | High (intermediary costs) | Low (just gas fees) |
| **Transparency** | Opaque | Fully public on-chain |
| **Speed** | Days for transfers | Seconds to minutes |
