# Getting Started

Gemach AI makes it easy to onboard and connect the wallets you need for DeFi interactions.

### Connecting Your Wallet

1. **Open the wallet selector:** In the top‑right corner of Gemach AI you’ll see a wallet icon showing your current network and balance. Click this icon to connect a new wallet.
2. **Choose a provider:** Select from popular providers such as MetaMask or WalletConnect for EVM chains, Phantom for Solana, Sui Wallet for the Sui network and any other supported wallets for emerging networks like Sonic or Berachain.
3. **Approve the connection:** Your wallet extension will prompt you to approve the connection and sign a message to prove ownership of the address. This signature is used to authenticate your session without exposing your private key.
4. **Session persistence:** Once connected, your session will remain active until you disconnect or clear your browser storage. You can connect multiple wallets and switch between them as needed.

> **Note:** Gemach AI is non‑custodial. The platform never has access to your private keys; all transactions require explicit approval through your wallet.

### Authentication Flow

Gemach AI uses a secure authentication flow:

* When you connect your wallet, you’re prompted to sign a message verifying ownership. No private keys are ever transmitted.
* The signed message establishes an encrypted session for subsequent interactions. You can safely close your browser and return later without re‑signing, as long as you do not disconnect.
* You can disconnect your wallet at any time from the wallet menu.

After connecting, you can begin using Chat, the Portfolio dashboard and AI Assistants with your linked wallets.
